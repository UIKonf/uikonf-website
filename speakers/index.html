Here are the speakers already confirmed for UIKonf 2015. More coming soon…

Graham Lee

Graham Lee works at Facebook, where he helps people make better tests so they can help people make better software. In the past he worked with some other people, and has written books and blogs so he can work with people he hasn't met too. His blog is at sicpers.info.

Mike Lee

Mike Lee is an American engineer living in Amsterdam. He has worked with some interesting and even historic project teams, including such titles and company names as Delicious Monster, Tap Tap Revenge, Appsterdam, Obama '08, Black Pixel, Lemurs Chemistry, and Apple. He is currently studying terraforming.

Maxim Cramer

Maxim currently works at SwiftKey where she had the privilege to be part of developing and launching SwiftKey Keyboard for iOS. Starting off with digital design at a young age, she grew curious about programming and so pursued it professionally, yet never straying far from UI/UX. Passionate about humans, she advocates testing products with people first and foremost, always enjoying the looks of surprise, confusion, and delight.

Natasha (the Robot) Murashev

Natasha is a Swift and WatchKit enthusiast who writes about her experiences with this still developing technology on natashatherobot.com and in her weekly Swift newsletter. She currently works as an iOS engineer for Capital One in San Francisco. In her free time, she works on personal projects, speaks at meetups and conferences, and contributes to open source.

Halle Winkler

Halle Winkler is the software developer and UX designer behind Berlin's Politepix, which produces OpenEars, the most popular offline speech recognition framework for iOS. She has worked in tech startups in the Silicons Alley, Valley and Allee since the days of Web .9 and has been preoccupied with human factors since day one. Her first line of code was in BASIC on a Commodore VIC-20, but since then there has often been a sample buffer involved.

Ash Furrow

Ash Furrow cut his teeth at 500px. Later, he moved on to Teehan+Lax, Canada's leading design agency. Now he works at Artsy, an amazing company whose goal it is to make art as popular and accessible as music. He is an open source enthusiast, author and co-author of several books, including "Functional Reactive Programming on iOS".

JP Simard

JP Simard is an Objective-C and Swift engineer at Realm, a modern mobile database replacement for SQLite and Core Data. He's the creator of jazzy (the documentation tool Apple forgot to release) and a co-organizer of SLUG, the largest Swift user group in the world.

Chris Eidhof

Chris Eidhof is a Dutch software developer living in Berlin. He spends most of his time building iOS and Mac apps, such as Deckset. He started UIKonf in 2013, objc.io, and wrote a book about Functional Programming in Swift.

Brian Gesiak

Brian is the creator of Quick, the Swift (and Objective-C) testing framework. He also maintains several other testing frameworks, is a software engineer at Facebook in New York, and was--in a former lifetime--a translator of Japanese literature. His blog is at http://modocache.io.

Saniul Ahmed

Saniul is an incurable optimist, whose bio puzzles government agencies all around the world. He currently works on Heyday by day, contributes to Prototope by night and spends weekends exploring the San Francisco Bay Area. Previously grew and lead the iOS team at NeoSphere in Moscow, studied in London and tinkered in Poland. As a life-long learner himself he strives to inspire new generations to do so too.

Claus Höfele

Claus currently works as Lead Engineer Mobile at eBay Classifieds Germany. He initiated the open-source app “Stolpersteine in Berlin” and is the author of CCHMapClusterController. His latest side project is “Here to There” – an app that shows nearby departures of public transport services.

Tom Adriaenssen

Tom is a freelance software engineer from Antwerp, Belgium. Currently working for iCapps, acting as the technical lead for their mobile developer team. Also known as the creator of ViewDeck. When not working, he co-manages a household together with his lovely wife, including 4 children and 2 cats. Notorious for using gifs when retorting on twitter.

Staters Gonna State - Eli Perkins

Eli is an iOS Engineer at Venmo in NYC, working to make payments an easier, more social experience. A craft beer aficionado, esports evangelist, and a perennial yak shaver, his passion in iOS lies in pixel perfection and elegant engineering. His explorations in Swift and functional programming in both Objective-C and Swift have piqued his curiosity as of late.

Hector Zarate

Hector Zarate is Spotify's own iOS cowboy. Before that, he was bouncing all across Europe like a pinball ball doing mobile applications for banks and other big companies. He writes on his blog iOSCowboy.com and enjoys submitting pull requests to open source projects on Github. He's been a mobile aficionado since his first Palm IIIe.

8 more speakers have been confirmed and will be announced in the coming days…
